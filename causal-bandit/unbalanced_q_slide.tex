\begin{itemize}
\item Need to trade of observing vs explicitly playing low probability arms.
\item Spend half our exploration time $h$ doing each.
\item Assume $q_i \in [0,\frac{1}{2}]$ and order variables such that $q_1 \leq q_2 \leq ... \leq q_N$ 
\item Let $m \in [2,N] = {\min i: q_i \geq \frac{1}{i}}$
\item Divide the arms into low probability $\{(i,1) : q_i < m\}$ and frequent $\{(i,0) \forall i  \cup (i,1) : q_i > m\}$ 
\item Divide the $h/2$ explicit play budget between the $m$ low probability arms, giving $\frac{h}{2m}$ samples each.
\item For the frequent arms, we expect $\sim q_i\frac{h}{2} \geq \frac{h}{2m}$ samples from the observe phase.
\end{itemize}
